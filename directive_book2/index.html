This example shows how directive works when scope is set to true.
By setting scope to true scope is not shared between
parent (controller) and the directive. Any change in the parent scope
stay in that scope only. Any change in the directive stay in the directive
scope only.

NOTE: If you press "Upgrade me" before pressing "Bob me", you will see "App 2.0" also in the
Directive scope. That is because directive will inherit scope from parent. After that,
when you press "Bob it", new scope.title variable will be assigned to the directive and any change
to the parent (ctrl) scope will not be reflected in the directive and vice versa.
